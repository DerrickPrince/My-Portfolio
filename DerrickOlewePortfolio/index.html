About Me

[Hello! I’m Derrick Olewe, a passionate development practitioner, project manager, and tech enthusiast. With a Master’s in International Development from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, I thrive at the intersection of sustainability, gender equality, technology, and data-driven decision-making. My journey so far has been grounded in community empowerment and social impact. I’ve led and contributed to projects like: HerStart Innovate the Future – A transformative initiative impacting 10,000 women across Tanzania, Ghana, and Uganda by providing entrepreneurship training, funding opportunities, and mentorship. BINTI Project – Empowering young mothers and girls affected by early marriages with life skills, financial literacy, reproductive health knowledge, and organic gardening practices. Urban Farming & Sustainable Agriculture – Driving food security and climate resilience through innovative and sustainable agricultural practices. I'm also deeply passionate about how data can inform sporting decisions, and I regularly explore global initiatives in areas like health, conflict resolution, and agricultural transformation—always looking for ways to adapt and improve solutions. When I’m not working or learning, I’m outdoors hiking, camping, or playing and watching football. I also enjoy painting, farming, and networking with fellow changemakers. Right now, I’m excitedly pursuing the Power Learn Project, a Pan-African initiative on a mission to unlock Africa's potential by training 1 million developers. Through accessible tech education, this project empowers young Africans to thrive in the global economy while driving innovation and sustainability—and I’m proud to be a part of it! My mantra? "Helping others help themselves." ]
